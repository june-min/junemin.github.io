I'm an undergraduate student majoring in Data Science and Big Data Technology at Jinan University.

My research interests include large language models (LLMs), machine learning, optimization, reasoning, and data mining. I am particularly interested in the application of LLMs to abstract reasoning, scientific problem solving, and intelligent decision-making systems.

My recent work focuses on abstract reasoning with large language models. I participated in ARC Prize 2025, where I explored LLM-based abstract reasoning frameworks built upon Mistral-NeMo-Minitron, incorporating LoRA fine-tuning, structured grid representations, tokenizer adaptation, and large-scale data augmentation techniques. This work was recognized with a Kaggle Silver Medal in ARC Prize 2025.

In addition, I have conducted research on optimization and machine learning applications, including urban waste transportation route optimization based on capacitated vehicle routing problems (CVRP), and prenatal diagnosis prediction using statistical analysis and machine learning models. These projects have won awards in the National Undergraduate Electrician Mathematical Modeling Competition and the China Undergraduate Mathematical Contest in Modeling (CUMCM).

My technical background includes Python, C++, MATLAB, SPSS, LoRA fine-tuning, large language model applications, optimization algorithms, machine learning, and data analysis. I also have experience with algorithm competitions and mathematical modeling.
